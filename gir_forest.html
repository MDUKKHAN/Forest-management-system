About Gir Forest

Gir Forest, located in the state of Gujarat, India, is renowned as the last abode of the Asiatic lion. Spanning over 1,412 square kilometers, the Gir Forest National Park and Wildlife Sanctuary is a jewel in India's natural heritage, offering a unique mix of dry deciduous forest, evergreen flora, and grasslands.

Established in 1965, Gir Forest plays a vital role in preserving the Asiatic lion's population, which was on the brink of extinction. Today, the forest stands as a testament to successful wildlife conservation efforts, drawing researchers, photographers, and tourists from across the globe.

Biodiversity

Gir Forest is home to over 400 species of plants, 300 species of birds, 30 species of mammals, and numerous reptiles. The iconic Asiatic lion is undoubtedly the highlight, but the forest also shelters leopards, antelopes, jackals, and crocodiles, making it a rich and diverse ecosystem.
