About Silent Valley

Nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, Silent Valley National Park is a pristine paradise of biodiversity. The park spans over 237 square kilometers and is home to lush forests, rare species, and unspoiled beauty. Its name originates from the eerie silence created by the absence of cicadas in the valley.

Key Attractions

Kunthi River

The Kunthi River flows through the park with crystal-clear waters, adding to its charm.

Rare Flora

Discover diverse vegetation, including tropical evergreen rainforests and medicinal plants.

Wildlife

Spot rare species such as the Lion-tailed Macaque, Nilgiri Tahr, and Malabar Giant Squirrel.

Untouched Trails

Explore serene trekking paths and immerse yourself in nature's tranquility.

Why is it Important?

Silent Valley National Park plays a critical role in conserving biodiversity. Its dense forests act as a carbon sink and regulate climate patterns. The park is also a hub for scientific research and eco-tourism, fostering sustainable development.
