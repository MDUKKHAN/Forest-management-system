About Corbett National Park

Established in 1936, **Jim Corbett National Park** is one of India's most celebrated wildlife reserves and the first park under the Project Tiger initiative. Located in Uttarakhand, the park spans over 1,300 square kilometers and is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers.

Named after the legendary hunter and conservationist Jim Corbett, the park offers picturesque landscapes, ranging from grasslands to riverine belts and dense forests.

Biodiversity

Corbett National Park boasts a rich ecosystem. It is home to the majestic Bengal tiger, elephants, leopards, crocodiles, and a variety of deer species. With over 600 bird species, it is also a paradise for bird watchers.
