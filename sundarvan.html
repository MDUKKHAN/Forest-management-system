About Sundarban Forest

Sundarban Forest, known for its breathtaking landscapes and rich biodiversity, is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. , Sundarvan Forest represents a unique geographical and ecological marvel. Its intricate network of rivers, tidal waterways, mudflats, and small islands is complemented by an incredible diversity of flora and fauna. The forest not only supports vibrant ecosystems but also serves as a critical habitat for globally endangered species.

The three core wildlife sanctuaries within the forest span over 139,700 hectares, making them vital breeding grounds for numerous species. As a part of a unique bioclimatic zone, Sundarvan Forest is celebrated for its high biodiversity and cultural heritage. The landscape, shaped by millennia of tidal activity, is a remarkable natural phenomenon, intertwined with myths and historical narratives that reflect its deep connection to human history.

This dense mangrove labyrinth, crisscrossed by brackish water channels, supports exceptional biodiversity. From rare flora to majestic wildlife, the Sundarvan Forest stands as a beacon of natural splendor. It is home to iconic creatures like the Royal Bengal Tiger, Ganges and Irrawaddy dolphins, estuarine crocodiles, and the critically endangered river terrapin (Batagur baska). As the only mangrove habitat for Panthera tigris tigris, it is a globally significant conservation area.

Biodiversity

A Sanctuary for Iconic Wildlife

Sundarvan Forest is renowned for being one of the most biologically rich ecosystems in the world. Its most famous resident, the Royal Bengal Tiger, reigns supreme as an apex predator, symbolizing the forest's vitality. Alongside tigers, the forest is teeming with wildlife, including spotted deer, wild boars, and Indian otters. These species form a complex food web, ensuring the forest’s ecological balance.

Aquatic Marvels of the Forest

The waterways of Sundarvan are equally astounding, brimming with aquatic biodiversity. From schools of fish to crabs and shrimp, these water channels support the livelihoods of local communities while maintaining the forest’s aquatic ecosystem. The presence of endangered species like the Ganges river dolphin and the estuarine crocodile underscores the forest's significance as a refuge for vulnerable species.

Vibrant Floral Ecosystems

The mangroves of Sundarvan Forest are a vital component of its ecosystem. These salt-tolerant plants act as natural buffers against storms and floods, safeguarding coastal areas. Beyond their ecological role, the forest is adorned with medicinal plants, vibrant orchids, and a variety of tropical flora that enhance both its natural beauty and economic importance.

Home to Diverse Birdlife

Sundarvan Forest is a bird watcher’s paradise, with numerous species finding refuge in its serene environment. From kingfishers to herons and migratory birds, the forest is alive with chirping melodies. This avian diversity plays a crucial role in pollination and seed dispersion, further supporting the forest’s health.
